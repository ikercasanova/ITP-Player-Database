# ITP Player Database — Performance Benchmark Sources

**Evidence-based benchmarks from European football academy research**
**Date: March 2026**

---

## Overview

This document provides the scientific basis for all performance test benchmarks used in the ITP Player Database. Each test section includes published reference data, derived threshold values per age group, confidence ratings, and full citations. The goal is to ensure every threshold shown to coaches, scouts, and players is grounded in peer-reviewed research from European football academies.

### Methodology

Thresholds were derived using the following approach:

1. **Primary sources** — Large-sample studies from German Bundesliga *Nachwuchsleistungszentren* (NLZ) were prioritized, as they are closest in context to 1. FC Koln's ITP program.
2. **Supporting sources** — Data from other top European academies (Spanish La Liga, Portuguese Primeira Liga, Austrian Bundesliga, Belgian Pro League, Aspire Academy) were used to validate and cross-reference.
3. **Threshold bands** — "Poor" represents below-average academy level, "Average" represents the mean for that age group, "Good" represents above-average / starter-level, and "Elite" represents top-tier / professional-level performance.
4. **Age groups** — U-17 (15-16 years), U-19 (17-18 years), U-21 (19-20 years).

### Threshold Derivation

The Hoppe et al. (2020) dataset from German Bundesliga NLZ serves as the primary reference — it is the largest and most contextually relevant study for 1. FC Koln's ITP program. Supporting European data validates the range.

- **"Poor"** is set approximately 1 SD below the NLZ mean, roughly matching lower-tier academy players or non-elite youth.
- **"Average"** represents the approximate NLZ mean for that age group.
- **"Good"** is set above the NLZ mean, roughly matching starter-level performance.
- **"Elite"** represents the upper end of the European academy distribution, matching professional-transition performance.

### Thresholds (higher is better)

| Age Group | Poor | Average | Good | Elite |
|-----------|------|---------|------|-------|
| U-17 | <34 cm | 34-38 cm | 39-42 cm | 43+ cm |
| U-19 | <36 cm | 36-40 cm | 41-44 cm | 45+ cm |
| U-21 | <38 cm | 38-42 cm | 43-47 cm | 48+ cm |

**Confidence: HIGH** — Large German NLZ sample (Hoppe et al., n=1,134), corroborated by multiple European studies. This is the most well-researched test in football academy literature.

## 2. Broad Jump (Standing Long Jump)

### Published Data

| Study | N | Age Group | Level | Mean +/- SD (cm) |
|-------|---|-----------|-------|-------------------|
| Vaeyens et al. (2006) | — | U16 | Belgian elite | 201.5 +/- 13.6 |
| Nowak et al. (2025) | — | Age 15 (P50) | Polish academy | 232 |
| Nowak et al. (2025) | — | Age 16 (P50) | Polish academy | 235 |
| Deprez et al. (2015) | — | U14-U18 | Belgian longitudinal model | Developmental curve |

### Threshold Derivation

Broad Jump data in European football academies is considerably sparser than CMJ data. The Belgian (Vaeyens, Deprez) and Polish (Nowak) studies provide the best available reference points. For U-19 and U-21, thresholds were estimated from developmental trajectory models and cross-referenced with NCAA Division I data (~241 cm as a college-level reference).

The wide variation across studies (Belgian U16 mean of 201.5 vs. Polish P50 of 235 at age 16) likely reflects differences in testing protocol, sample selection, and population. Thresholds were set conservatively to account for this.

### Thresholds (higher is better)

| Age Group | Poor | Average | Good | Elite |
|-----------|------|---------|------|-------|
| U-17 | <190 cm | 190-210 cm | 211-230 cm | 231+ cm |
| U-19 | <200 cm | 200-220 cm | 221-240 cm | 241+ cm |
| U-21 | <210 cm | 210-230 cm | 231-250 cm | 251+ cm |

**Confidence: MEDIUM (U-17), LOW (U-19 / U-21)** — Limited European football-specific data. U-19 and U-21 thresholds are partially estimated from developmental curves and cross-sport references. More Bundesliga NLZ data would strengthen these values.

## 3. 5m Sprint

### Published Data

| Study | N | Age Group | Level | Mean +/- SD (s) |
|-------|---|-----------|-------|------------------|
| Gonaus et al. (2019) | 261-998 per age | U15-U19 | Austrian Bundesliga NLZ | Age-specific norms |
| Bujnovsky et al. (2019) | — | U17 | Czech First League | ~1.10-1.16 |
| Ruf et al. (2024) | — | U11-U19 | TSG Hoffenheim (Bundesliga NLZ) | 5m centile data |

### Threshold Derivation

5m sprint data is relatively scarce in football literature because many academies test 10m or 20m rather than 5m as their short-distance benchmark. The Gonaus et al. (2019) Austrian NLZ dataset is the most powerful source (hundreds of athletes per age group). The Ruf et al. (2024) Hoffenheim data is particularly valuable as a direct Bundesliga NLZ reference.

Performance plateaus around age 16-17 for the 5m distance, meaning U-19 and U-21 thresholds are relatively close together. Best estimates for academy means: U17 ~1.03s, U19 ~1.03s, U21 ~1.02s.

### Thresholds (lower is better)

| Age Group | Poor | Average | Good | Elite |
|-----------|------|---------|------|-------|
| U-17 | >1.15 s | 1.08-1.15 s | 1.02-1.07 s | <1.02 s |
| U-19 | >1.12 s | 1.05-1.12 s | 0.99-1.04 s | <0.99 s |
| U-21 | >1.10 s | 1.03-1.10 s | 0.97-1.02 s | <0.97 s |

**Confidence: MEDIUM** — The Austrian NLZ (Gonaus) and Hoffenheim (Ruf) datasets are strong, but 5m is not universally tested across European academies. The narrow time window (fractions of a second) makes threshold placement more sensitive to measurement error.

## 4. 10m Sprint

### Published Data

| Study | N | Age Group | Level | Mean +/- SD (s) |
|-------|---|-----------|-------|------------------|
| Gonaus et al. (2019) | 261-998 per age | U15-U19 | Austrian Bundesliga NLZ | Age-specific norms |

| Mendez-Villanueva & Buchheit (2011) | — | U13-U18 | Aspire Academy (Qatar) | Age-specific data |

### Threshold Derivation

The Gonaus et al. (2019) Austrian NLZ dataset is the most powerful source for 10m sprint, with hundreds of athletes per age group across multiple seasons. This was the primary source for threshold derivation. Czech First League (Bujnovsky) and Aspire Academy (Mendez-Villanueva/Buchheit) data served as cross-references.

Best estimates for academy means: U17 ~1.77s, U19 ~1.75s, U21 ~1.74s.

### Thresholds (lower is better)

| Age Group | Poor | Average | Good | Elite |
|-----------|------|---------|------|-------|
| U-17 | >1.88 s | 1.80-1.88 s | 1.74-1.79 s | <1.74 s |
| U-19 | >1.84 s | 1.76-1.84 s | 1.70-1.75 s | <1.70 s |
| U-21 | >1.82 s | 1.74-1.82 s | 1.68-1.73 s | <1.68 s |

**Confidence: HIGH** — The Austrian NLZ dataset (Gonaus et al.) provides robust, large-sample norms. 10m sprint is one of the most commonly tested distances in European football academies, and multiple studies converge on similar values.

## 6. 30-15 Intermittent Fitness Test (IFT) — VIFT

### Published Data

| Study | N | Age Group | Level | Mean +/- SD (km/h) |
|-------|---|-----------|-------|---------------------|
| Buchheit et al. (2013) | — | U16 | Aspire Academy | 19.2 +/- 0.9 |
| Buchheit et al. (2013) | — | U18 | Aspire Academy | 19.7 +/- 1.0 |
| Fernandez Javega & Pena-Gonzalez (2024) | — | U15 | Spanish youth | 18.99 +/- 1.31 |
| Fernandez Javega & Pena-Gonzalez (2024) | — | U16 | Spanish youth | 18.81 +/- 1.55 |
| Silva et al. (2022) | — | U14-U19 | Turkish youth | 17.44-19.54 (progressive) |
| Buchheit (2021) | — | Elite male | General benchmark | ~19.5 |

### Threshold Derivation

The 30-15 IFT is widely used in European football but has less published normative data specific to German NLZ than physical performance tests. No German Bundesliga NLZ-specific 30-15 IFT data exists in the literature, as the DFB uses a different testing battery for its academies.

The Buchheit et al. (2013) Aspire Academy data is the most cited reference. Spanish (Fernandez Javega & Pena-Gonzalez) and Turkish (Silva) studies provide supporting age-group data. Buchheit's (2021) general benchmark of ~19.5 km/h for elite males provides an anchor point.

Thresholds reflect the progressive improvement from U-17 to U-21, with the elite threshold set at levels consistent with professional or near-professional aerobic fitness.

### Thresholds (higher is better)

| Age Group | Poor | Average | Good | Elite |
|-----------|------|---------|------|-------|
| U-17 | <17.5 km/h | 17.5-19.0 km/h | 19.1-20.5 km/h | >20.5 km/h |
| U-19 | <18.5 km/h | 18.5-20.0 km/h | 20.1-21.5 km/h | >21.5 km/h |
| U-21 | <19.0 km/h | 19.0-20.5 km/h | 20.6-22.0 km/h | >22.0 km/h |

**Confidence: MEDIUM** — Good reference data from Aspire Academy and multiple European studies, but no German NLZ-specific norms. The 30-15 IFT protocol is well-standardized, which improves cross-study comparability, but club-specific training loads and testing conditions can influence results.

## Notes for Future Updates

- **Priority improvement:** Obtain Broad Jump data from German Bundesliga NLZ to strengthen the currently low-confidence U-19/U-21 thresholds.
- **30-15 IFT:** If 1. FC Koln ITP begins collecting 30-15 IFT data systematically, internal norms should eventually supplement published benchmarks.
- **Custom thresholds:** The ITP Player Database supports coach-customized thresholds that override these defaults. Coaches should adjust based on their own squad data once sufficient testing sessions have been collected.
- **Measurement standardization:** Thresholds assume standardized testing conditions (indoor timing gates for sprints, force platform or contact mat for CMJ). Non-standard setups may introduce systematic bias.
